The 17 Sustainable Development Goals

The Sustainable Development Goals (SDG) or World Goals are 17 points established in 2015 by the Member States of the United Nations. The intention of these is that, in a period of 15 years, that is to say in 2030, has intervened in problems such as poverty, care for the planet and guaranteeing peace and prosperity. These objectives are designed taking into account that, if an area is intervened, this can affect the other. Therefore, we must focus on finding a balance in the sustainability of all of them, both environmental and economic and social.
